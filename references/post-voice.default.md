# Post Voice and Style

The voice and rules every post obeys - the content analogue of the document DESIGN
system. `fcxo-post` reads this file, applies it, and appends durable feedback to
"Learned from feedback" so it sharpens with each use. This is the baseline the plugin
ships; `fcxo-post` copies it to `practice/me/<Owner> - Voice.md` and replaces the
defaults with the user's real voice as it becomes clear (from their positioning, their
past posts, or a brainstorm).

## Voice

- Senior, plain, specific. Write the way the user would say it to a peer across a table.
- One real, usable idea per post: a lens, a decision rule, a lesson from real work.
- Concrete over generic: a number, a situation, what actually happened carries the post.
- Confident and plain. The post earns attention by being worth reading.

## Structure (the reader's journey)

- Open on the fact or the situation. No throat-clearing ("In this post...", "I want to share...").
- One connected thought: each sentence extends the one before it.
- A real payoff: the reader leaves with something usable. End on a concrete point or a callback.

## Do

- Ground every post in the user's real experience.
- Short, plain sentences. Define any jargon on first use.
- Anonymize clients and private detail.

## Don't (the AI-slop tells)

- Rule-of-three staccato ("Fast. Simple. Effective.").
- Broetry (one sentence per line with blank lines to farm clicks).
- Significance-announcers ("Here's the thing.", "What stood out to me...").
- Formulaic aphoristic closers ("That's the shift happening right now.").
- Forced emoji, hashtag stacks, engagement bait ("DM me", "save this for later").
- LLM / corporate vocab (delve, leverage, unlock, supercharge, dive deep, tapestry, realm).
- The em-dash (long dash); use a hyphen or two sentences.

## Format

- Default channel LinkedIn; short and scannable, in real paragraphs.
- Hashtags: default none; at most a few plain ones, only when one is genuinely useful.
